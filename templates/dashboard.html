Engine RPM

Engine RPM stands for "Revolutions Per Minute" and indicates how fast the engine is running.

> 5000 RPM: High rpm = Higher fuel consumption
1000 - 3000 RPM: Ideal range
< 1000 RPM : Idling your car for too long wastes fuel

Vehicle Speed

The faster a vehicle travels, the more air it must push out of the way, which can increase aerodynamic drag and require more energy from the engine to maintain the speed.

> 50 MPH: Higher speeds significantly reduce fuel economy.
50 MPH: Ideal speed for most cars
< 50 MPH : Low speeds (especially < 20mph) are good but not ideal.

Driving Dynamics

Rapid acceleration and harsh braking can reduce fuel efficiency, while smooth, gradual acceleration and deceleration can help maximize fuel economy. Coasting, or letting the vehicle slow down naturally by taking the foot off the accelerator, can also save fuel.

Rapid acceleration can cause the engine to work harder, burning more fuel to achieve the desired speed.
Coasting or letting the vehicle slow down naturally by taking the foot off the accelerator, can also save fuel.
Harsh braking can waste energy by converting the vehicle's momentum into heat, which is dissipated through the brakes.
